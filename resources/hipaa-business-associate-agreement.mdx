# Business Associate Agreement

This Business Associate Agreement (the "BAA") is entered into as of the date of last signature below (the "Effective Date") by and between:

- **Covered Entity:** [Customer legal name], a [entity type] organized under the laws of [jurisdiction] ("Covered Entity"); and
- **Business Associate:** Tembo Data Systems, Inc., a Delaware corporation ("Tembo" or "Business Associate").

Covered Entity and Business Associate are each a "Party" and together are the "Parties." This BAA forms part of the agreement, order form, terms of service, data processing addendum, statement of work, or other written agreement governing Covered Entity's use of Tembo's services (the "Agreement"). If Covered Entity is itself a business associate of a covered entity, references to "Covered Entity" include Covered Entity acting as an upstream business associate, as applicable.

## Background

A. Covered Entity is a "covered entity" or "business associate" as those terms are defined under the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996, as amended by the Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health Act and their implementing regulations (collectively, "HIPAA").

B. Tembo provides an AI coding agent platform and related hosted, self-hosted, support, integration, and professional services under the Agreement (the "Services").

C. In providing the Services, Tembo may create, receive, maintain, or transmit Protected Health Information on behalf of Covered Entity.

D. The Parties intend for this BAA to satisfy the requirements applicable to business associate agreements under HIPAA, including 45 CFR §§ 164.502(e) and 164.504(e).

For good and valuable consideration, the Parties agree as follows.

## 1. Definitions

Capitalized terms not defined in this BAA have the meaning given to them under HIPAA.

1. **Breach** means the acquisition, access, use, or disclosure of Protected Health Information in a manner not permitted under the Privacy Rule that compromises the security or privacy of the Protected Health Information, as defined at 45 CFR § 164.402.
2. **Designated Record Set** has the meaning given to that term at 45 CFR § 164.501.
3. **Electronic Protected Health Information** or **ePHI** means Protected Health Information maintained in or transmitted by electronic media.
4. **HHS** means the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services.
5. **Individual** has the meaning given to that term at 45 CFR § 160.103 and includes a personal representative under 45 CFR § 164.502(g).
6. **Privacy Rule** means 45 CFR Part 160 and Part 164, Subparts A and E.
7. **Protected Health Information** or **PHI** has the meaning given to that term at 45 CFR § 160.103, limited to PHI created, received, maintained, or transmitted by Tembo from or on behalf of Covered Entity.
8. **Security Incident** has the meaning given to that term at 45 CFR § 164.304.
9. **Security Rule** means 45 CFR Part 160 and Part 164, Subparts A and C.
10. **Subcontractor** has the meaning given to that term at 45 CFR § 160.103 and includes any agent or subcontractor that creates, receives, maintains, or transmits PHI on behalf of Tembo.
11. **Unsecured PHI** has the meaning given to "unsecured protected health information" at 45 CFR § 164.402.

## 2. Permitted Uses and Disclosures by Tembo

Tembo may use or disclose PHI only as follows:

1. To perform the Services and other obligations under the Agreement.
2. As permitted or required by this BAA or the Agreement, provided that the use or disclosure would not violate HIPAA if performed by Covered Entity.
3. As required by law.
4. For Tembo's proper management and administration or to carry out Tembo's legal responsibilities, provided that any disclosure for these purposes is either required by law or Tembo obtains reasonable written assurances that the recipient will keep the PHI confidential, use or further disclose it only as required by law or for the purpose for which it was disclosed, and notify Tembo of any known breach of confidentiality.
5. To report violations of law to appropriate federal or state authorities consistent with 45 CFR § 164.502(j)(1).

Tembo will not sell PHI, use PHI for marketing, or use PHI in any manner prohibited by HIPAA without Covered Entity's prior written authorization. Tembo will not use PHI to train foundation models or other general-purpose AI models except to the extent expressly authorized in the Agreement or in written instructions from Covered Entity.

## 3. Minimum Necessary Standard

Tembo will use, disclose, and request only the minimum necessary PHI to accomplish the intended purpose, unless an exception to the minimum necessary standard applies under HIPAA. Covered Entity is responsible for configuring its use of the Services and limiting PHI submitted to Tembo to the minimum necessary for the applicable workflow.

## 4. Safeguards

Tembo will implement and maintain appropriate administrative, physical, and technical safeguards designed to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of ePHI that Tembo creates, receives, maintains, or transmits on behalf of Covered Entity. These safeguards will include measures reasonably designed to prevent uses or disclosures of PHI other than as permitted by this BAA and to comply with the Security Rule requirements applicable to business associates.

## 5. Reporting Impermissible Uses, Disclosures, and Security Incidents

Tembo will report to Covered Entity any use or disclosure of PHI not permitted by this BAA and any Security Incident involving ePHI of which Tembo becomes aware. Tembo will make the report without unreasonable delay and no later than five business days after discovery.

The Parties acknowledge that unsuccessful Security Incidents, including pings, port scans, failed login attempts, and other routine unsuccessful attacks that do not result in unauthorized access, use, disclosure, modification, or destruction of ePHI, may occur frequently. Tembo is not required to provide separate notice for these unsuccessful Security Incidents unless required by law, but this paragraph serves as ongoing notice of their occurrence.

## 6. Breach Notification

Tembo will notify Covered Entity of any Breach of Unsecured PHI without unreasonable delay and no later than 30 calendar days after discovery. To the extent known at the time of notice, Tembo's notice will include:

1. A brief description of the Breach, including the date of the Breach and the date of discovery.
2. The types of Unsecured PHI involved.
3. The Individuals affected or reasonably believed to be affected.
4. Steps Tembo has taken or plans to take to investigate, mitigate harm, and protect against further Breaches.
5. Information reasonably requested by Covered Entity to meet Covered Entity's notification obligations under HIPAA.

Covered Entity is responsible for determining whether notification to Individuals, HHS, the media, or other parties is required, except to the extent the Parties agree otherwise in writing.

## 7. Mitigation

Tembo will take reasonable steps to mitigate, to the extent practicable, any harmful effect known to Tembo resulting from a use or disclosure of PHI by Tembo or its Subcontractors in violation of this BAA.

## 8. Subcontractors

Tembo will ensure that any Subcontractor that creates, receives, maintains, or transmits PHI on behalf of Tembo agrees in writing to restrictions, conditions, and safeguards that are at least as protective of PHI as those in this BAA. Tembo remains responsible for Subcontractor compliance with the applicable requirements of this BAA.

Tembo may use Subcontractors to provide the Services, including hosting, infrastructure, model provider, monitoring, support, and integration providers, provided that such Subcontractors are bound by written obligations consistent with HIPAA where required.

## 9. Access to PHI

If Tembo maintains PHI in a Designated Record Set, Tembo will make that PHI available to Covered Entity as reasonably necessary for Covered Entity to satisfy its obligations under 45 CFR § 164.524. If an Individual requests access to PHI directly from Tembo, Tembo will forward the request to Covered Entity within ten business days. Covered Entity is responsible for responding to Individual access requests.

## 10. Amendment of PHI

If Tembo maintains PHI in a Designated Record Set, Tembo will make PHI available for amendment and incorporate amendments as reasonably directed by Covered Entity in accordance with 45 CFR § 164.526. If an Individual requests amendment of PHI directly from Tembo, Tembo will forward the request to Covered Entity within ten business days. Covered Entity is responsible for responding to Individual amendment requests.

## 11. Accounting of Disclosures

Tembo will document disclosures of PHI as required for Covered Entity to respond to requests for an accounting of disclosures under 45 CFR § 164.528. Upon Covered Entity's written request, Tembo will provide information reasonably necessary for Covered Entity to respond to such requests, including, to the extent applicable and available:

1. The date of disclosure.
2. The name and, if known, address of the recipient.
3. A brief description of the PHI disclosed.
4. A brief statement of the purpose of the disclosure or a copy of the written request for disclosure.

Tembo will provide this information within ten business days after Covered Entity's written request, unless a longer period is agreed by the Parties or permitted by law.

## 12. Availability of Books and Records

Tembo will make its internal practices, books, and records relating to the use and disclosure of PHI available to HHS for purposes of determining compliance with HIPAA.

## 13. Covered Entity Obligations

Covered Entity will:

1. Notify Tembo of any limitation in Covered Entity's notice of privacy practices that may affect Tembo's use or disclosure of PHI.
2. Notify Tembo of any changes in, or revocation of, permission by an Individual to use or disclose PHI, to the extent the change may affect Tembo's use or disclosure of PHI.
3. Notify Tembo of any restriction on the use or disclosure of PHI that Covered Entity has agreed to or is required to comply with under 45 CFR § 164.522, to the extent the restriction may affect Tembo's use or disclosure of PHI.
4. Not request Tembo to use or disclose PHI in a manner that would violate HIPAA if performed by Covered Entity.
5. Obtain any consents, authorizations, and permissions required for Tembo to provide the Services and process PHI as contemplated by the Agreement and this BAA.

## 14. Data Ownership

As between the Parties, Covered Entity retains all rights in PHI submitted to Tembo under the Agreement. Tembo's stewardship, processing, or storage of PHI does not transfer ownership of PHI to Tembo.

## 15. Return or Destruction of PHI

Upon termination of the Agreement or this BAA, Tembo will return or destroy PHI maintained by Tembo, if feasible and as directed by Covered Entity. This obligation applies to PHI in the possession of Tembo's Subcontractors.

If return or destruction is not feasible, Tembo will notify Covered Entity of the reason return or destruction is infeasible, extend the protections of this BAA to the retained PHI, and limit further uses and disclosures to those purposes that make return or destruction infeasible. This section survives termination of this BAA.

## 16. Term and Termination

This BAA begins on the Effective Date and remains in effect until all PHI maintained by Tembo on behalf of Covered Entity is returned or destroyed, or until the Parties agree that return or destruction is infeasible and the protections of this BAA continue as described above.

Either Party may terminate this BAA and the Agreement for cause if the other Party materially breaches this BAA and fails to cure the breach within 30 calendar days after receiving written notice. If cure is not possible, the non-breaching Party may terminate immediately. If termination is not feasible, the non-breaching Party may report the breach to HHS.

## 17. Relationship to Agreement

This BAA is incorporated into and forms part of the Agreement. If there is a conflict between this BAA and the Agreement regarding PHI, this BAA controls. Except as expressly stated in this BAA or required by law, this BAA does not create rights in any third party.

## 18. Regulatory References

References to HIPAA statutes or regulations are references to those provisions as amended or replaced from time to time.

## 19. Amendment

The Parties will negotiate in good faith to amend this BAA as necessary to comply with changes in HIPAA or other applicable law. This BAA may be modified only by a written amendment signed by authorized representatives of both Parties.
